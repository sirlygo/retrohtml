Host prep

Run RetroBat at home

  • Power on your RetroBat PC and launch the frontend.
  • Start your streaming host (Sunshine/Parsec/Steam Remote Play).
  • Open/forward the streaming port via your router or a VPN tunnel.
  • Plug in your controllers or enable virtual gamepads.
  • Protect access with strong credentials and 2FA where available.

Connection notes

Stay in control

Use a VPN or reverse proxy instead of exposing raw ports when possible. For the best latency, keep your host on wired Ethernet.

Know what’s legal

RetroBat is a frontend. Only load ROMs and BIOS files you dumped yourself. This page doesn’t distribute content and can’t fetch any for you.

Quick start

Set up access in three steps

  1. Host. Launch RetroBat, start your streaming app, and verify you can connect locally.
  2. Secure. Use Tailscale/ZeroTier, a reverse proxy, or SSH tunneling to create a private URL.
  3. Connect. Paste the URL above, hit “Launch session,” and play.

Troubleshooting

  • Low framerate

    Lower the stream bitrate/resolution in your host app and favor hardware encoding (NVENC/AMF/Quick Sync).

  • Controller not detected

    Enable gamepad passthrough in your streamer. Chrome/Edge support the Gamepad API when the tab is active.

  • Black screen

    Ensure RetroBat is running on a GPU output. Headless rigs may need a dummy HDMI dongle.
